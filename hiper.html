Hiper

Problem Statement

People need a way to get in touch with professionals in personal development, get help, get coaching, get mentoring and to get inspiration especially when they are going through a difficult period or experiencing an unpleasant event. Because they wish to vaincre difficulties in their daily life, to have better mental health, to succeed in all the areas of their life and to become a better person. We will know this to be true when we see an increase in the number of subscribed users and an increase in paid services by the users who need experts.

Project Duration

18 Weeks

Possible Solution

We believe that by creating an app to connect the users with professionals in personal development and providing a database of audio/video content in personal development, we will be able to answer the expectation of the users and set our app apart from the competitors.

My Role

Information architecture, UX Researcher, UX Designer, UI Designer, Product Manager

Tools Used

Adobe XD, Miro, Visual Paradigm, Optimal Workshop, Marvel and FlowMapp

Demographic target

18 to 60

  • Want to reach out professionals to get counseling on personal development.
  • Need to deal with difficult events in life.
  • Need to find inner balance.
  • Look for content on personal development.

    • Getting advice from people who are experts in a domain and who are willing to help will help oneself develop and achieve higher goals. At the same time, it's a good way to create a virtual network to get into contact with more people in the same domain. Our Expert app "Hiper" will be an responsive and interactive app which allows people to get into contact with experts in personal development domain to flourish in their personal and professional life.

    User Survey

    Quantitative Research

    88 French speaking people answered to the online Survey, all aged between 18 to 60 years old with 8 people prior to 60 years old.

    Key Takeaway
  • Many people don't have a clear idea about the definition of personal development and wellbeing.
  • A lot of people don't know they need help to improve their well-being or better develop themselves to pursue their goals.
  • The survey and user interview have both confirmed my hypothesis about the ages of people and their needs.
  • Thanks to this research, I have a clear idea of the features and characteristics in the application.
  • People have strong needs in personal development and well-being. It's very difficult to have loyal users at the beginning. Offering valuable content can help tremendously.

    • User Interviews

    Qualitative Research

    Interview Goals
  • Confirm our hypothesis and targets.
  • Understand the needs and habits of the potential users.
  • Determine the main features.
  • Understand the pain-points.

    • I gathered a lot of valuable data during the interviews. They helped me to refine all the initial ideas and hypothesis that I had at the beginning.

    Key Takeaway
  • It's highly interesting to see that the needs and goals of the users and professional users are very in common.
  • Most people having problems in life don't know what's the real problem they need to deal with and have a lot of difficulties to find the right professional practitioner to help them.
  • To attract both normal users and professionals and keep them motivated to use the app that we're going to conceive, we need to make the app trustful and bring real interests to all the users.

    User Journey Map

    To define user personas is very important to know the needs and pain-points of the potential users. But these data remain static. It's also important to analyze the dynamic data of the potential users, for which I worked out a user Journey map for each user persona.

    User Flow

    Once I worked out the User Journey Maps, I put myself in the skin of the user personas and defined the workflows.

    While defining the workflow, I followed the principles of Dr. Norman:

  • Visibility: Users should know, just by looking at an interface, what their options are and how to access them.
  • Feedback: The user must receive feedback after every action they perform to let them know whether or not their action was successful.
  • Affordance: Affordance is the link between how things look and how theyâ€™re used.
  • Mapping: Mapping is the idea that, in a good design, the controls for something will closely resemble their effect.
  • Constraints: Constraints restrict a particular form of user interaction with an interface.
  • Consistency: People learn new things and manage better when they recognize patterns.

    • Card Flowing

    To establish a logical structure of the application, select the most essential features and respect the users' expectation, I conducted card sorting with 8 potential users.

    Site Map

    Based on the card sorting result, I worked out a site map of my appllication.

    Design Language System

    Once I selected the colors of the application and had a stable prototype version, I worked out a design language system to align all the visual principles to respect.

    Usability Test

    Design and test are two steps tightly connected. Without performing usability test, the design cannot be improved according to the users preference. When I did my design, for each version of my design, I conducted usability test sessions in an interative way with potential users to collect feedback. The feedback has helped me tremendously in the process of enhance the level of my design.

    Usability Issues

    According to the feedback of the users, I performed modifications for each iteration and achieved the final design.

    1. Add progression bar on the onboarding questions page.

    2. Add contrast in the map.

    3. Add more personalized content on the home page.

    4. Rearrange the practitioner profile page.

    5. Reformat the offer page.

    6. Redefine the practitioner's calendar.
